MY FAVORITE SCRIPTURES

Alama 29:9

"I know that which the Lord hath commanded me, and I glory in it. I do not glory of myself, but I glory in that which the Lord hath commanded me; yea, and this is my glory, that perhaps I may be an instrument in the hands of God to bring some soul to repentance; and this is my joy."

Alma 56:47

"Now they never had fought, yet they did not fear death; and they did think more upon the liberty of their fathers than they did upon their lives; yea, they had been taught by their mothers, that if they did not doubt, God would deliver them."

Moroni 7:45

"And charity suffereth long, and is kind, and envieth not, and is not puffed up, seeketh not her own, is not easily provoked, thinketh no evil, and rejoiceth not in iniquity but rejoiceth in the truth, beareth all things, believeth all things, hopeth all things, endureth all things."

3 Nephi 11:13

"And it came to pass that the multitude went forth, and thrust their hands into his side, and did feel the prints of the nails in his hands and in his feet; and this they did do, going forth one by one until they had all gone forth, and did see with their eyes and did feel with their hands, and did know of a surety and did bear record, that it was he, of whom it was written by the prophets, that should come."

I truly know that the Book of Mormon is the word of God. One of my favorite verses is Alma 29:9. In Alma 29:9, Alma expresses his joy in bringing others to Christ and fulfilling God's sacred purposes. Similarly, my testimony is strengthened by the joy and peace the Book of Mormon brings into my life as it teaches of Christ. This sacred text provides guidance, comfort, and inspiration, helping me draw closer to our Savior. Through its divine messages, I feel the love of our Heavenly Father and gain a deeper understanding of His plan for us.
-Koa Eldredge
The Book of Mormon has profoundly impacted my life through its powerful teachings of faith, hope, and the love of Jesus Christ. One of my favorite scriptures is Moroni 10:4-5, which invites all to pray with real intent to know of its truthfulness. This scripture has strengthened my personal faith and provided guidance in times of uncertainty. The Book of Mormon is a constant source of spiritual enlightenment and peace, reinforcing my belief in God's plan and His love for each of us. It's a testament to the enduring power of scripture in our lives today."
-Enoch Nawahine
I know that the Book of Mormon is the word of God and has the power to change lives. I know that the more we read its words, the more we can know our savior and understand his sacrifice and love for us. I know that by studying the Book of Mormon we can feel peace, joy, comfort, love, and any other feeling we are searching for. I know that the Book of Mormon truly is a testament of Gods pure love for his children.
-Kanai Eldredge
I have a testimony of the Book of Mormon and the principles that we learn from it. I’m grateful for my knowledge of the book and the faith/ hope it has brought to my life. I finished the Book of Mormon for the first time on my mission and I knew without a shadow of a doubt it was true. I know that as I continuously study the Book of Mormon on a consistent basis the Lord will bless me with his spirit to make decisions. I know that to be true because I’ve felt it.
-Raider Damuni
I know the Book of mormon is true, and the spiritual power I feel as I open the pages is unlike something I've never felt before. One of my favorite verses is Moroni's promise in the last chapter. The reason for that is because every time I ask my Heavenly Father for confirmation, I feel the heavens open, and my heart suddenly warms up and beats faster. I love the truth it brings into my life, and I have truly felt God speaking to me through it. It indeed is the keystone to my religion. I say these things in the name of Jesus Christ , Amen.
Sariah Torres
The Book of Mormon is one of the few things in my life that I can always count on to keep me on track. No matter how long I go without reading, or how far away from Christ I constantly drift, I can always trust in the power of the Book of Mormon to bring me right back. What I've come to understand through my experience is that the transformative power of the Book of Mormon manifests itself most profoundly when we actively immerse ourselves in its teachings. It has been, and always will be one of the greatest blessings of my life!
-Kwinton Ho Ching
